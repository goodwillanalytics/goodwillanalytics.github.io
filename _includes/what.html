Our Story

Goodwill Analytics all started with one idea: for data scientists to lend their superpowers to social cause organisations, in order to show them how to make the best use of their data.

Many social cause organisations gather large amounts of data over time, but may not have the funding or the resources to make use of it. Social causes often find themselves collecting mountains of data but are unable to unlock its full potential.

And thatâ€™s where we step in.

We select a number of social causes with specific data problems that a handful of skilled volunteers will tackle during one action-packed Goodwill Analytics event.

Each social cause organisation is assigned a dedicated Data Ambassador who works closely with them in the lead-up to the event. They work as consultants to understand of their organisational challenges and to gather as much information as possible about their data and the problems they face.

At the event, we open the doors to skilled volunteers from diverse backgrounds and levels of experience. Volunteers get the chance to collaborate with each other and work closely with one social cause in small groups led by a Data Ambassador.

In a nutshell, volunteers apply their technical skills to draw insights, suggestions and solutions from each social cause's data and supplemented by publicly available information. The insight and solutions provided to each social cause gives them with an understanding of the power of their own data. It has the potential to reshape the way they operate and has a hugely beneficial impact upon the causes they support.

Donating money is a fantastic way of supporting social causes. However, donating time and skills can greatly improve the way that a social cause operates by optimising their systems and helping them to make better use of the donations they receive.
